The operational gap

Good tools can still leave resilience decisions fragmented.

The cockpit idea is not that monitoring, ticketing, vendor reporting, backup tooling, or DR documentation are missing. The opportunity is synthesis: turning scattered operational evidence into a governed leadership view.

Incident status takes coordination effort

Status updates, ownership, cadence, impact, RCA actions, and executive summaries can require manual stitching across several systems.

SLA and vendor exposure needs one view

Open escalations, SLA clocks, evidence confidence, vendor responses, and accountable owners become more useful when visible together.

Governance must be designed in

AI summaries, RBAC, audit logs, source confidence, and no-PII boundaries must be first-class controls, not afterthoughts.

The suggestion

A read-only resilience cockpit above existing systems.

The Banking Systems Resilience Cockpit would aggregate operational signals from monitoring, ITSM/ticketing, vendor SLA evidence, backup tooling, DR/BCP checklists, and incident/RCA records into a role-based coordination layer.

The cockpit would not write to core banking systems. For MVP and pilot validation, it would rely on mock, manual, or read-only integrations, making the concept easier to evaluate without weakening security or operational control.

  • ✓Complement current monitoring, ITSM, backup, vendor, and DR tools.
  • ✓Use source adapters so external schemas do not leak into the operating model.
  • ✓Keep AI assistance local, non-PII, approval-gated, and fully auditable.
  • ✓Make evidence confidence visible in operational and executive views.

Pilot roadmap

A cautious path from concept to evidence.

The proposed sequence starts with low-risk validation, then moves toward operational proof only when source, governance, and adoption risks are controlled.

Validate source boundaries

Identify initial monitoring, ITSM, vendor, backup, and DR/BCP evidence sources that can be represented safely in read-only or simulated form.

Build mock/manual MVP

Prototype the cockpit views with manual evidence entry and mocked source adapters before seeking deeper integration.

Run controlled pilot

Measure incident-status preparation time, escalation visibility, SLA breach coverage, RCA tracking, and stakeholder adoption.

Review executive value

Use pilot evidence to decide whether to extend integrations, narrow scope, or stop before unnecessary platform investment.

Bank-safe principles

Respect the control environment from day one.

No core-banking writes

The cockpit is a coordination layer; all core banking state changes remain outside its scope.

No customer PII in AI inputs

AI summary requests must exclude customer names, account data, transactions, card/PIN/authentication data, secrets, and tokens.

Every action audit-ready

Role claims, permission decisions, source confidence, approvals, and operational changes should remain traceable.
